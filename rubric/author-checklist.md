# OC4IDS Field-Level Mapping Report — Author Submission Checklist

**Version:** v1.0
**Audience:** Country teams preparing a field-level mapping report for CoST MSG / government review
**Purpose:** Confirm the report contains the minimum evidence a reviewer needs before submission. Reports missing any of these items are returned at triage without full review.

---

## How to use this checklist

Tick every box before submitting. If you cannot tick an item, explain in your cover note why it is absent. A report returned for missing checklist items is considered *not yet submitted* — no scoring is recorded until resubmission passes triage.

---

## The 16 items

### Quantitative evidence

- [ ] **1. Quantified headline.** The executive summary states a percentage of required OC4IDS fields currently populated. Adjectives like *significant gaps* or *largely complete* do not satisfy this item.
- [ ] **5. Template version.** The OC4IDS version used is stated explicitly (e.g. `0.9.5`, `0.9.6`). Flag if a deprecated version was used.
- [ ] **6. Coverage by phase.** Coverage percentages are reported per lifecycle phase (identification, preparation, procurement, implementation, completion, maintenance, decommissioning).
- [ ] **7. Every required field has a status.** Each required OC4IDS field carries one of: Populated / Partial / Missing / Not applicable. NA entries carry a justification.
- [ ] **8. Source-field provenance.** Every mapped OC4IDS field traces back to a named source-system field. Where no source field exists, the report states so explicitly.

### Scope and methodology

- [ ] **2. Portal URLs and access dates.** Every portal or system URL reviewed is listed with the date(s) it was accessed.
- [ ] **3. Sample defined.** The project sample is characterised by count, aggregate value, sectors, time window, and sampling method.

### Legal anchoring

- [ ] **4. Procurement law and ATI/FOI law cited.** Both the public procurement law and the access-to-information / FOI framework are cited with section references. OGP commitments and sector rules are added where relevant.

### Diagnosis

- [ ] **9. Gap root causes.** Every material gap is classified by the 8-category typology: (1) no data exists, (2) data exists outside the reviewed system, (3) exists only in unstructured documents, (4) poor quality, (5) cannot be linked across systems, (6) collected but not publicly disclosed, (7) blocked by workflow/legal constraints, (8) in country context but not in template.
- [ ] **10. Data beyond OC4IDS.** Fields the country already discloses that fall outside OC4IDS are examined, with a recommendation on retention as extensions or upstream feedback to OCP.

### Data quality

- [ ] **11. Codelist alignment.** The report states which source-system codelists align with OC4IDS codelists directly and which need translation.
- [ ] **12. Document URL health.** A sample of document URLs has been tested and the percentage that resolve is reported. Minimum sample size: 20 documents or 10% of the document set, whichever is greater.

### Action

- [ ] **13. Recommendations with owners and timelines.** Every recommendation names its owner (role plus person where possible) and gives a timeline. *"KADPPA should strengthen data collection"* fails. *"The KADPPA Director of IT will automate OC4IDS export from the e-GP system by Q3 2026"* passes.
- [ ] **14. Roadmap phasing.** An implementation roadmap splits actions into short-term (0–3 months), medium-term (3–12 months), long-term (12+ months).
- [ ] **16. Decision summary panel.** One page or boxed section classifies publishable elements as: can publish now / can publish after light transformation / requires system change / requires legal or institutional action.

### Evidence base

- [ ] **15. Mapping template annexed.** The completed OC4IDS field-level mapping template (the 6-sheet Excel workbook) is attached to the report.

---

## Writing discipline

The following applies across the whole report. A report with persistent writing-discipline failures is returned for copy-editing before review.

- Active voice wherever reasonable.
- No em dashes (use commas or full stops).
- Quantitative claims carry the evidence behind them.
- Adjective-only claims (*significant, notable, virtual, substantial*) are replaced with numbers or removed.
- Every finding cites a source: the template, a document, a legal reference, or an interview.

---

## What happens next

On submission, a reviewer runs the 10-item **Reviewer Gate Check**. Reports that pass the gate (10 / 10) proceed to a full weighted review on the 100-point rubric. Reports that fail (≤9 / 10) are returned with a comment letter listing the gaps.

Gate score 2 / 10 is the published floor (Kaduna calibration). If you are below 6 / 10 on this checklist before submitting, revise before you send.
